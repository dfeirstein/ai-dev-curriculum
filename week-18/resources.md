# Week 18 Resources

Resources for speed building, time management, and shipping under constraints. Use these when you want to go deeper on any topic from this week.

---

## Speed Building and Shipping

- **[Ship It (Levels.io)](https://levels.io/how-i-build-my-minimum-viable-products/)** -- How Pieter Levels builds and ships products rapidly. His approach to MVPs and speed aligns directly with this week's methodology.
- **[The Cult of Done Manifesto](https://medium.com/@bre/the-cult-of-done-manifesto-724ca1c2ff13)** -- Thirteen principles about finishing things. Worth reading before Day 1.
- **[Shape Up: Appetite (Basecamp)](https://basecamp.com/shapeup/1.2-chapter-03)** -- The concept of "appetite" -- deciding how much time something is worth before you start, not after. Your appetite this week is 5 days.

---

## Time Management for Builders

- **[Maker's Schedule (Paul Graham)](http://www.paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html)** -- Why builders need large blocks of uninterrupted time. Plan your build days accordingly.
- **[Timeboxing (Harvard Business Review)](https://hbr.org/2018/12/how-timeboxing-works-and-why-it-will-make-you-more-productive)** -- The research behind time-boxing. Why constraints make you more productive, not less.

## A Note on Speed

Speed is a skill, not a talent. The first time you try to build something in 5 days, it feels rushed. The tenth time, it feels natural. The key insight from this week isn't "build fast." It's "build efficiently." Efficiency comes from knowing your tools, making decisions quickly, and focusing on what matters. Everything else follows.
