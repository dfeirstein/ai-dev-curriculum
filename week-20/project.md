# Project: Retrospective, Polish, and Launch

**What you're building:** Your final deliverables -- a retrospective, a polished portfolio, and a plan for what's next.

**Time estimate:** 8-10 hours across the week

**What you'll need open:** Ghostty, your browser, something to write with

---

## The Brief

This is the final week. You'll reflect on what you've built, polish everything one last time, write a plan for the future, and share your work with the world.

No new features. No new projects. Just reflection, polish, and launch.

---

## Phase 1: The Retrospective (Days 1-2)

### Step 1: Write Your Retrospective

This is the most important deliverable of the week. Write an honest retrospective covering your full 20-week journey.

Answer these questions:

**What you built:**
- List every project and what it does
- Which one are you most proud of? Why?
- Which one taught you the most?

**What you learned:**
- What concepts clicked early? Which took longer?
- What surprised you about building software?
- What's something you understand now that you didn't at Week 10?

**What was hard:**
- What was the most frustrating moment?
- When did you feel most lost?
- How did you push through it?

**How your relationship with AI changed:**
- How did your prompts evolve from Week 1 to Week 20?
- What do you know now about directing AI that you didn't know at the start?
- When did Claude Code give you something genuinely wrong? How did you handle it?

**What's next:**
- What do you want to build next?
- What skill do you want to develop further?
- How will you use what you've learned?

Write at least 1,000 words. Don't worry about it being perfect on the first draft. Get the ideas down, then refine.

### Step 2: Edit and Refine

Use Claude Code to help polish your writing:

> "Review this retrospective. Improve the clarity and flow. Don't change my voice -- just make the writing tighter. Suggest any areas where I should add more detail or where I'm being too vague."

Read it aloud. Does it sound like you? Does it tell the story of your journey honestly?

### Step 3: Publish the Retrospective

Add it to your portfolio site as a blog post:

> "Add this retrospective as a blog post on my portfolio site. Title it '[Your Title]'. Add a publication date and estimated reading time."

Deploy the updated site.

Visit every project and page in your portfolio one final time.

**Portfolio site:**
- [ ] Home page loads quickly and looks polished
- [ ] About page is accurate and up to date
- [ ] All project links work (live demos and GitHub)
- [ ] All screenshots display correctly
- [ ] Blog section has at least two posts (Week 19 post + retrospective)

**TeamTask Pro:**

**Independent project:**
- [ ] Live demo works -- core feature functions correctly
- [ ] README is professional and complete
- [ ] Screenshots are current

**Speed build:**
- [ ] Live demo works -- core feature functions correctly
- [ ] README is professional and complete

### Step 5: Fix Any Issues

If anything is broken or outdated, fix it now:

> "Fix [specific issue] in [project name]."

This is your last polish pass. After this, everything should be production-ready and presentation-worthy.

### Step 6: Update Your GitHub Profile

If you haven't already, create a GitHub profile README:

> "Write a GitHub profile README for me. I'm an AI-native builder who has shipped [X] production applications in 20 weeks. Include a brief intro, links to my top projects, and my tech stack. Keep it concise and professional."

## Phase 3: Plan What's Next (Day 4)

### Step 7: Write Your "What's Next" Plan

The curriculum ends, but your building doesn't. Write a concrete plan for the next month:

**What will you build?**
Pick one project to start within the next two weeks. Not "someday." A specific idea with a specific start date.

**What will you learn?**
Pick one new skill area from the guide (React Native, AI/ML concepts, system design, a specific domain, design skills). How will you learn it? What's the first step?

**How will you stay active?**
- Will you build in public? Where? (Twitter/X, blog, LinkedIn)
- Will you contribute to open source regularly?
- Will you write about what you're building?

**Career direction:**
- Are you pursuing freelance work? If so, what's your first outreach?
- Are you job hunting? If so, what companies interest you?
- Are you building a startup? If so, what's the MVP?
- Are you continuing to learn? If so, what's the next curriculum or course?

Write this plan down. Commit to it. Share it with someone who will hold you accountable.

### Step 8: Set Up for the Future

If your plan involves any of these, set them up now:

- **Freelance:** Create profiles on relevant platforms. Write your bio using your portfolio.
- **Job hunting:** Update LinkedIn. Add your projects. Set up job alerts.
- **Building in public:** Create or update your Twitter/X bio. Write a first post sharing your portfolio.
- **Open source:** Star and watch projects you want to contribute to. Set a recurring reminder to check for issues.

Preparation now means momentum later.

---

## Phase 4: Go Public (Day 5)

### Step 9: Share Your Portfolio

This is the moment. Share your work with the world.

**Share on Twitter/X:**
Write a post about your journey. Something like: "I just completed 20 weeks of building software with AI. Here's what I shipped: [list projects]. Here's my portfolio: [link]. I'm an AI-native builder, and I'm looking for [what you're looking for]."

**Share on LinkedIn:**
Write a more professional version. Focus on the skills you've developed and the projects you've shipped.

**Share with people you know:**
Send your portfolio link to 5 people: friends, family, mentors, former colleagues. Ask for feedback.

**Post in relevant communities:**
Share in developer communities, AI communities, or communities related to what you're building.

### Step 10: Celebrate

You finished a 20-week curriculum. You went from zero to multiple production applications. You learned to direct AI to build professional software. You have a portfolio, a blog, and a body of work.

That's not a small thing. That's a transformation. However you celebrate -- take a moment to acknowledge what you've accomplished.
