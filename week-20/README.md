# Week 20: The AI-Native Developer

**Theme:** Reflect. Plan ahead. Launch your career.

This is the final week.

Twenty weeks ago, you opened a terminal for the first time. You didn't know what a commit was, what an API did, or how a website got from someone's computer to the internet. You had never directed an AI to build anything.

Now look at what you have:

- **A personal landing page** -- your first project, live on the internet.
- **A portfolio site** -- with a blog, SEO, and polished design.
- **TeamTask Pro** -- a multi-tenant SaaS with authentication, Stripe payments, notifications, dashboards, testing, monitoring, and CI/CD. A real product.
- **An independent project** -- your idea, your decisions, production-ready.
- **A speed build** -- proof you can ship a complete application in 5 days.
- **An open source contribution or npm package** -- proof you can work beyond your own projects.
- **A professional portfolio** -- showcasing all of it, with documentation, demos, and writing.

You built all of this by directing AI. Not by writing code. By researching, planning, executing, verifying, and shipping. That's not just a skill. That's a career.

This week is about reflection, planning, and launching.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Reflect on what you've built and learned** -- the full journey, honestly.
2. **Articulate your identity** as an AI-native builder.
3. **Plan your next chapter** -- what will you build, learn, and pursue next?
4. **Share your work publicly** -- put yourself out there.

## What You Ship

- A published retrospective (blog post)
- Final portfolio polish
- A "what's next" plan
- Your portfolio shared publicly

## Time Commitment

~8-10 hours across the week. Lighter on building, heavier on thinking and writing.

- **Weeks 1-19 completed.** Your portfolio is assembled and all projects are live.

Congratulations. Now go build something.
