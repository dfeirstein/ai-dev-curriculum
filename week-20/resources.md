# Week 20 Resources

Resources for continuing your journey as an AI-native builder. These aren't for this week specifically -- they're for everything that comes after.

---

## Staying Current with AI Development

- **[Anthropic Blog](https://www.anthropic.com/blog)** -- Updates on Claude and AI research. Follow this for changes to your primary tool.
- **[Claude Code Documentation](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/claude-code/overview)** -- The official docs, which are updated as new features are added. Check back periodically.
- **[Vercel Blog](https://vercel.com/blog)** -- Updates on Next.js, the AI SDK, and the broader React ecosystem.
- **[The Pragmatic Engineer](https://blog.pragmaticengineer.com/)** -- Gergely Orosz's newsletter covering software engineering trends, including AI's impact on development.

---

## Career Development

- **[Levels.fyi](https://www.levels.fyi/)** -- Compensation data for tech roles. Useful for understanding the market if you're job hunting.
- **[Y Combinator Startup Library](https://www.ycombinator.com/library)** -- Free resources on starting a company. If the founder path interests you, start here.
- **[Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/)** -- Community of solo builders and small startup founders. Good for finding like-minded people and learning from their experiences.
- **[Upwork](https://www.upwork.com/)** -- Freelance platform. If freelancing interests you, start here with small projects to build reputation and reviews.

---

## Learning Paths

### Mobile Development
- **[React Native Documentation](https://reactnative.dev/docs/getting-started)** -- The official docs. Since you know React (through Next.js), React Native will feel familiar.
- **[Expo Documentation](https://docs.expo.dev/)** -- A framework built on top of React Native that simplifies development. Recommended starting point for mobile.

### AI/ML Concepts
- **[3Blue1Brown: Neural Networks](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi)** -- Visual, intuitive explanations of how neural networks work. No math prerequisites.
- **[Anthropic: Introduction to AI Safety](https://www.anthropic.com/research)** -- Understanding AI safety and alignment. Relevant context for building with AI.
- **[LangChain Documentation](https://docs.langchain.com/)** -- A framework for building applications with language models. The next step if you want to build AI-powered features.

### System Design
- **[System Design Primer](https://github.com/donnemartin/system-design-primer)** -- A comprehensive guide to system design concepts. Useful as your projects grow in complexity.
- **[ByteByteGo](https://bytebytego.com/)** -- Visual explanations of system design concepts. Approachable and practical.

### Design
- **[Refactoring UI](https://www.refactoringui.com/)** -- Practical design tips for developers. Focuses on making things look good without being a designer.
- **[Laws of UX](https://lawsofux.com/)** -- Key principles of user experience design. Understanding these helps you make better product decisions.

## Building in Public

- **[Building in Public Guide (Kevon Cheung)](https://www.buildinpublic.com/)** -- A practical guide to sharing your work openly.
- **[Show HN (Hacker News)](https://news.ycombinator.com/showhn.html)** -- Share your projects with the tech community. Can drive traffic and feedback.
- **[Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com/)** -- Launch your projects and get feedback from a tech-savvy audience.

---

## Writing

- **[Paul Graham's Essays](http://www.paulgraham.com/articles.html)** -- Essays on startups, technology, and building things. "Do Things That Don't Scale" and "Maker's Schedule" are particularly relevant.
- **[Hemingway App](https://hemingwayapp.com/)** -- A tool for making your writing clearer and more concise. Useful for blog posts and README editing.

---

## Communities

- **[Dev.to](https://dev.to/)** -- Developer community with articles, discussions, and a supportive culture.
- **[Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/)** -- Tech news and discussion. High signal, sometimes intense.
- **[r/webdev (Reddit)](https://www.reddit.com/r/webdev/)** -- Web development community. Good for staying current on trends and getting feedback.
- **[Discord: Next.js](https://nextjs.org/discord)** -- The Next.js community Discord. Useful for specific framework questions and staying current.

---

## Books Worth Reading

- **[The Pragmatic Programmer](https://pragprog.com/titles/tpp20/the-pragmatic-programmer-20th-anniversary-edition/)** -- Timeless advice on software development as a craft. The concepts apply regardless of whether you write code by hand or direct AI.
- **[Shape Up (Basecamp)](https://basecamp.com/shapeup)** -- A free book on how to scope, plan, and ship software projects. Directly applicable to how you work.
- **[Zero to One (Peter Thiel)](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18050143-zero-to-one)** -- On building something new. Relevant if you're thinking about starting something of your own.
