# Week 7 Resources

Reference material for portfolio design, content strategy, SEO, and MDX. Focus on understanding concepts and finding inspiration.

---

## Portfolio Inspiration

- [Brittany Chiang](https://brittanychiang.com/) -- Clean, well-structured developer portfolio. Notice the project showcase format.
- [Lee Robinson](https://leerob.io/) -- VP of Product at Vercel. Simple portfolio with an excellent blog. Built with Next.js.
- [Josh Comeau](https://www.joshwcomeau.com/) -- Creative, interactive portfolio with a strong blog. Notice how personality comes through.
- [Minimal Portfolio Examples on Dribbble](https://dribbble.com/search/developer-portfolio) -- Visual inspiration for layout and design direction.

## SEO

- [Google SEO Starter Guide](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide) -- Official guide from Google. Covers the basics of how search engines find and rank pages.
- [Open Graph Protocol](https://ogp.me/) -- The standard for social media preview cards. Short read that explains what OG tags are and why they matter.
- [opengraph.xyz](https://www.opengraph.xyz/) -- Test your OG tags by entering your URL. See exactly what your link preview looks like on social platforms.
- [Next.js Metadata](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/building-your-application/optimizing/metadata) -- How Next.js handles metadata, OG tags, and sitemaps. The framework makes this straightforward.

## MDX

- [MDX Documentation](https://mdxjs.com/) -- Official MDX documentation. Focus on the "What is MDX?" section for the conceptual overview.
- [Next.js MDX Support](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/building-your-application/configuring/mdx) -- How to use MDX with Next.js. Multiple approaches explained.
- [Content Layer](https://contentlayer.dev/) -- A library that makes working with MDX content in Next.js cleaner. Worth understanding as an option.
- [Velite](https://velite.js.org/) -- A newer content layer alternative. Simpler configuration, good Next.js support.

## Content and Writing

- [Paul Graham -- Write Like You Talk](http://www.paulgraham.com/talk.html) -- Short essay on writing clearly. Good advice for blog posts.
- [Building in Public](https://www.buildinpublic.com/) -- Community and resources for sharing your work openly.
- [Hemingway Editor](https://hemingwayapp.com/) -- Paste your writing in to check readability. Aims for clear, concise prose.

## Design

- [Tailwind CSS Typography Plugin](https://tailwindcss.com/docs/typography-plugin) -- The `@tailwindcss/typography` plugin styles prose content beautifully. Essential for blog posts.
- [shadcn/ui Themes](https://ui.shadcn.com/themes) -- Theme customization for shadcn/ui components. Pick colors that match your personal brand.
- [Font Pairing Resources](https://fontpair.co/) -- If you want custom fonts, this helps you find combinations that work together.
- [Realtime Colors](https://www.realtimecolors.com/) -- Visualize color palettes on a real page layout. Helpful for choosing your portfolio's color scheme.

## Custom Domains

- [Vercel Custom Domains Guide](https://vercel.com/docs/projects/domains) -- Step-by-step instructions for connecting a domain to Vercel.
- [Namecheap](https://www.namecheap.com/) -- Popular domain registrar with competitive prices.
- [Cloudflare Registrar](https://www.cloudflare.com/products/registrar/) -- Sells domains at wholesale cost. Also provides DNS and CDN services.

## Performance

- [Lighthouse](https://developer.chrome.com/docs/lighthouse/) -- Google's built-in performance auditing tool. Available in Chrome DevTools.
- [Next.js Image Optimization](https://nextjs.org/docs/app/building-your-application/optimizing/images) -- How Next.js handles image optimization automatically. Important for a fast portfolio.
- [Web Vitals](https://web.dev/vitals/) -- The metrics Google uses to measure page experience. Good to understand conceptually.
