# Week 15: Project 3 -- Choose Your Own (Week 1 of 2)

**Theme:** Pick a problem. Research it. Plan it. Build the MVP.

You've built a landing page, a portfolio, a bookmark manager, and an entire multi-tenant SaaS with auth, payments, notifications, dashboards, testing, monitoring, and CI/CD. You have a complete toolkit and a proven workflow.

Now it's time to build something that's entirely your idea. Not a guided project. Not a curriculum exercise. Something you choose because you'd actually use it, or because it solves a problem you've experienced firsthand.

This is a two-week project. Week 15 is research, planning, and building the MVP. Week 16 is polish, testing, monitoring, and launch.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Independently scope a project** -- decide what to build and what "done" looks like.
2. **Conduct competitive research** -- understand what exists and where the opportunity is.
3. **Prioritize features** -- distinguish must-haves from nice-to-haves.
4. **Make architecture decisions** -- choose which tools from your stack this project actually needs.
5. **Execute a build sprint** -- go from zero to working MVP in a few days.

## What You Ship

A **deployed MVP** of your chosen project. Core functionality works. It's live on the internet. It doesn't need to be polished -- that's next week. It needs to work.

## Time Commitment

~15-20 hours across the week. The research and planning phase is just as important as the building phase. Don't rush it.

## Prerequisites

- **Weeks 1-14 completed.** You have the full stack knowledge and a proven workflow.
- A GitHub account and Vercel account.
- An idea for what you want to build (or willingness to pick one from the suggestions in project.md).

## How to Approach This Week

1. **Read guide.md** carefully. This week is about decision-making as much as building.
2. **Pick your project** and commit to it. Don't spend three days choosing -- spend one day max, then go.
3. **Research before you build.** Look at competitors. Map out features. Plan your data model.
4. **Build through Claude Code** using the workflow you've practiced for 14 weeks.

The best project ideas come from your own experience. What app do you wish existed? What tool would make your life easier? What problem have you noticed that nobody has solved well? Start there.
