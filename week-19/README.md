# Week 19: Portfolio Assembly

**Theme:** Package everything you've built into a professional portfolio.

You've built a lot over the past 18 weeks. A personal landing page. A portfolio site. A bookmark manager. TeamTask Pro -- a full multi-tenant SaaS. An independent project. A speed build. An open source contribution or npm package. That's a body of work most developers take years to accumulate.

This week, you assemble it all into a cohesive, professional portfolio. Not just a list of links. A curated presentation of who you are, what you can build, and how you think. This is the artifact that represents you to the world -- to potential employers, clients, collaborators, or anyone who wants to know what you're capable of.

---

## Learning Objectives

1. **Curate your work** -- select and present your best projects strategically.
2. **Write effective project documentation** -- READMEs that make people want to try your apps.
3. **Create compelling demos** -- videos and screenshots that showcase your work.
4. **Present yourself as an AI-native builder** -- tell your story clearly.
5. **Think about marketing** -- the meta-skill of presenting your skills to the world.

## What You Ship

A **complete, polished portfolio** with:
- Updated portfolio site showcasing all projects
- Polished READMEs for every project
- Screenshots and live demo links
- Demo videos for your top 2 projects
- At least one blog post about your journey or a specific project
- All live demos verified and working

## Time Commitment

~10-12 hours across the week.

## Prerequisites

- **Weeks 1-18 completed.** You have multiple shipped projects.
- Your portfolio site from Week 7.
- All projects deployed on Vercel.

## How to Approach This Week

1. **Read guide.md** to understand what makes portfolios effective.
2. **Audit all your projects first.** What works? What's broken? What needs updating?
3. **Work through project.md** systematically. Polish each project, then update the portfolio site.
4. **Write the blog post.** This is not optional. Writing about your work is a force multiplier.

This week is about presentation, not building. You've already built everything. Now you're packaging it so the world can see what you've accomplished.
